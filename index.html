    • I show a bar chart that shows 10 years with the most amount of games. This range can be selected from the slider next on the left side of the dashboard. When hovering over the slider, the user can pick their own range and see top 10 years with the most amount of total games.
    • I calculate the winning percentage by dividing the number of wins and half the number of ties by the total number of games they played. Then I create a bar chart that shows the highest 10 of those ratios by country.
    • I use the same winning percentage only on World Cup games. However, I also calculate each teams rivals’ winning percentage average to see how likely they are to win the World Cup next time. Winning percentage can be vastly different if a team was doing well just by not playing against the best teams. If a team’s winning percentage is higher compared to its rivals, we should view that as a lower risk. Hence, the lower the risk is the bigger the dots get on the graph. This is to eliminate bias just by winning percentage. If a smaller dot is higher than a big dot, than predicting based on past history for the team represented by the smaller dot might be riskier than its winning percentage shows. So the size of the dots are essentially a risk measuring tool to bet based on winning averages.
  • D3 is good for visualizing data for multiple reasons. One thing I liked was not having to generate an SVG by hard-coding it.
